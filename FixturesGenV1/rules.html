🏸 CITI Badminton Tournament

Welcome to the CITI Badminton League. Please review the rules before fixtures begin.

"Serve, Smile, Socialize. Repeat."

👥 Teams & Players
  • 5 Teams participate.
  • Each team has 8/7 players (5/4 Men + 3 Women).
🔄 Tournament Format
  • Categories: MD, WD, XD.
  • MD & XD → play every team twice.
  • WD → play every team once.
  • Top 3 teams per category qualify for knockouts.
  • A team may qualify in one category even if not qualified in others.
Knockout Flow
  • Qualifier: 1st vs 2nd → Winner to Final
  • Eliminator: 3rd vs Qualifier Loser
  • Final: Qualifier Winner vs Eliminator Winner
📋 Pair Submission
  • Fixtures shared by 02 March 2026.
  • Pairings must be submitted by 06 March 2026 – 6 PM.
  • Submitted pairs cannot be changed.
👨 Men's Doubles Rules
  • 5 Men → all players must partner each other (10 unique pairs).
  • Only ONE pair may repeat once in the tournament.
  • If 4 men → each pair max 2 matches.
  • Same pair cannot play two knockout matches.
👫 Mixed Doubles Rules
  • With 5 men:
    • 4 men → max 2 matches each
    • 1 man → may play 3 matches
  • Women → max 4 XD matches.
  • All pairings must be unique.
👩 Women's Doubles Rules
  • Each unique pair max 3 matches together.
  • Same pair cannot appear in multiple knockout matches.
🧮 Scoring System
League Stage
  • Single set → 21 points.
  • Win = 2 points | Loss = 0 points.
  • Tie breaker → highest score difference.
Knockouts
  • Best of 3 sets.
  • Each set played to 15 points.
⭐ Knockout Bonus Points

Knockout points are added to league points for overall standings.

Match Winner Loser
Qualifier 5 Points 2 Points
Eliminator 2 Points 1 Point
Final 10 Points 5 Points
🏆 Overall Champion Criteria
  • Team must win at least one Final to be eligible.
  • Highest number of Final wins → Champion.
  • If tied → Highest total points (League + Knockout).
  • Next eligible team becomes Runner-Up.
⏱ General Rules
  • Players cannot play two categories in the same knockout round.
  • Match delay beyond 10 minutes may result in walkover.
  • Non-marking shoes recommended (barefoot allowed).
  • Organizer decisions are final and binding.
